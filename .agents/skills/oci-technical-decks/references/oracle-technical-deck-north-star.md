# Oracle Technical Deck North Star

Use this file when the deck should feel like an Oracle technical presentation rather than a presales storyline.

These guidelines were derived from the attached Oracle technical reference decks without reusing their visible content.

## Core Feel

- Instructor-led, not self-reading.
- Confident and technical, not salesy.
- Large, bold headlines with plenty of whitespace.
- Strong contrast between dark section slides and light content slides.
- One hero device per slide: architecture, comparison table, diagram, screenshot, or map.

## Cover And Section Slides

- Prefer a dark charcoal or deep blue background with subtle texture.
- Keep the title stack left-aligned.
- Use a short subtitle or session framing line under the title.
- Use restrained Oracle-style abstract shapes on the right or top-right as atmosphere, not decoration everywhere.
- Keep metadata sparse: speaker, org, date, or deck label only when useful.

## Content Slides

- Use a light background for most technical content.
- Keep titles large, bold, and left aligned.
- Use an optional short subtitle when it helps frame the question.
- Give diagrams and tables generous breathing room.
- Favor two-column explanation patterns:
  - bullets or short statements on one side
  - architecture, diagram, screenshot, or comparison on the other

## Common Technical Visual Devices

- Comparison tables with dark header rows and light body cells.
- Side-by-side screenshots or terminal captures with strong labels.
- Architecture or flow diagrams paired with short explanatory bullets.
- Summary or takeaway bands only when the message is audience-facing. Presenter-only interpretation belongs in notes.
- Q&A or section-break slides using the dark inverse treatment.

## Footer Treatment

- Keep page number and copyright understated.
- Use Oracle brand markers sparingly.
- Include internal or confidential labels only when the deck is explicitly internal and approved to carry them.

## Palette Direction

- Charcoal or deep blue for section slides.
- Warm white or light neutral for content slides.
- Oracle red and muted gold as controlled accents.
- Cool blue or teal for technical illustration highlights.
- Avoid rainbow palettes or random card colors.

## Typography And Density

- Headline first, explanation second.
- Avoid `6+` bullet lists unless the slide is truly reference-heavy.
- Avoid document paragraphs except on abstract, legal, or required policy slides.
- If a concept needs a paragraph to be understood, move the explanation to presenter notes.

## Instructor-Led Rule

Every slide should include presenter notes covering:

- what question the slide answers
- what to emphasize verbally
- what confusion to preempt
- how to transition to the next slide

## What To Avoid

- visible speaker prompts or presenter coaching
- copied internal footers or confidentiality labels when the deck should be customer-safe
- tiny screenshots
- dense tables that require unreadable font sizes
- decorative backgrounds on ordinary content slides
- too many visual centers competing on one slide
