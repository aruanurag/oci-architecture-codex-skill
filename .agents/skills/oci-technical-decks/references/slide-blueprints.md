# Technical Slide Blueprints

Use this file after you know the deck mode.

## 1. Cover

Purpose:

- frame the topic
- set the technical depth

Pattern:

- dark cover or section style
- strong title
- short subtitle
- minimal metadata

## 2. Topic Framing

Purpose:

- define the technical problem or question

Pattern:

- clear title
- short framing statement
- `2-4` pain points, constraints, or design goals

## 3. Service Or Capability Overview

Purpose:

- explain what the OCI capability is and where it fits

Pattern:

- title
- short subtitle
- one architecture or component visual
- `3-4` supporting statements

## 4. Mechanism Or Flow

Purpose:

- explain how the service works

Pattern:

- flow, packet path, control path, or processing diagram
- short left-side or top-side explanation
- notes carry the deeper walkthrough

## 5. Comparison Table

Purpose:

- compare modes, shapes, generations, or service choices

Pattern:

- strong comparison title
- limited rows
- clear tradeoff columns
- presenter notes explain nuance that should not overload the table

## 6. Deep Dive

Purpose:

- unpack one subsystem, component, or operator concern

Pattern:

- one component title
- one diagram, screenshot, or annotated table
- `2-3` explanation bullets

## 7. Use Cases And Workload Fit

Purpose:

- connect the mechanism to real OCI workloads

Pattern:

- `3-4` workload examples
- short rationale for each
- honest caveats when the fit is conditional

## 8. Operations And Tradeoffs

Purpose:

- explain deployment, observability, performance, limits, or prerequisites

Pattern:

- short comparison or checklist
- one visible takeaway
- deeper guidance in notes

## 9. Architecture Placement

Purpose:

- show where the capability sits in a broader OCI architecture

Pattern:

- one architecture slide
- short callouts
- one interpretation line or notes-based explanation

## 10. Summary

Purpose:

- reinforce what the audience should remember

Pattern:

- `3` concise takeaways
- one recommended next conversation, lab, or workshop step

## 11. Q&A

Purpose:

- close the lesson cleanly

Pattern:

- dark section style
- minimal on-canvas text
- presenter notes may include backup prompts or appendix references

## 12. Footprint Or Coverage

Purpose:

- show region presence, service reach, platform footprint, or deployment coverage

Pattern:

- strong title
- one large map, footprint visual, or availability frame
- short legend or callout treatment
- keep the slide visually quiet so the footprint is easy to scan
