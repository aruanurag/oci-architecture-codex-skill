# Deck Blueprints

Use this file after you know the audience and the engagement type.

## 1. Executive Briefing

Use for executive sponsors, line-of-business leaders, or mixed customer leadership audiences.

Default length: `6-8` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Title and account context
2. Why now
3. Current-state pain and business impact
4. Recommended OCI approach
5. One architecture or platform view
6. Expected business value
7. Adoption path or engagement plan
8. Next steps

Good visuals:

- one simplified architecture
- one capability map
- one value summary

Avoid:

- detailed implementation steps
- too many service icons
- appendix material in the core deck

## 2. Customer Solution Recommendation

Use when the audience includes architects, platform owners, or technical decision makers and the deck needs a stronger technical narrative.

Default length: `8-12` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Title
2. Executive summary
3. Requirements and decision criteria
4. Current-state landscape
5. Recommended target-state approach
6. OCI architecture
7. Security, operations, and governance considerations
8. Migration or implementation path
9. Tradeoffs and alternatives
10. Value summary
11. Risks, assumptions, and dependencies
12. Next steps

## 3. Workshop Readout

Use after discovery, assessment, or architecture workshops.

Default length: `5-7` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Workshop objective and scope
2. Key findings
3. Prioritized recommendations
4. Target architecture or platform direction
5. Risks and dependencies
6. Proposed action plan
7. Next working session or decision needed

## 4. POV or POC Proposal

Use when the goal is to secure agreement on a structured validation effort.

Default length: `6-9` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Problem statement
2. Hypothesis and success criteria
3. Proposed OCI solution
4. Scope: in and out
5. Demo flow or architecture view
6. Timeline and milestones
7. Customer inputs and dependencies
8. Exit criteria
9. Next steps

## 5. Competitive Positioning

Use when the deck needs to explain why OCI is the better fit for the customer's situation.

Default length: `5-7` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Customer goals and decision criteria
2. Recommended OCI posture
3. Differentiators mapped to those criteria
4. Migration or adoption risk mitigation
5. Tradeoffs and honest boundaries
6. Proposed engagement path
7. Next steps

Keep this customer-specific. Do not turn it into a generic vendor comparison grid unless the user explicitly asks for one.

## 6. Internal Account Strategy

Use for internal alignment with account teams, specialists, or leadership before the customer meeting.

Default length: `5-7` slides.

Suggested flow:

1. Account situation
2. Opportunity thesis
3. Customer stakeholders and likely objections
4. Recommended OCI narrative
5. Proposed architecture or platform angle
6. Risks and open questions
7. Meeting objective and call plan

## Slide Patterns

Use these patterns inside any of the blueprints above.

### Recommendation Slide

- Title states the recommendation directly
- Three supporting points maximum
- One clear tradeoff or assumption if needed

### Architecture Slide

- One diagram only
- Two to four callouts
- One takeaway sentence

### Comparison Slide

- Compare against customer decision criteria
- Keep OCI strengths tied to the workload
- Show tradeoffs honestly

### Next-Step Slide

- Decision needed
- Proposed owner
- Timing
- Customer input required
