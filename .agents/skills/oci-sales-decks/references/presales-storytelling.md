# Presales Storytelling

Use this file when the deck needs a stronger narrative, sharper audience targeting, or better recommendation language.

## Core Story Arc

Build the story in this order:

1. Why now
2. Current-state friction
3. Desired future state
4. Recommended OCI approach
5. Why OCI for this customer
6. Delivery path
7. Risks, dependencies, and assumptions
8. Next step

If a slide does not help one of those moves, it is probably appendix material.

## Audience Modes

### Executive Sponsor

Optimize for:

- business impact
- speed to outcome
- risk reduction
- decision clarity

Keep technical detail light and use technical slides as proof, not as the main narrative.

### Technical Decision Maker

Optimize for:

- architecture fit
- security and operational posture
- migration path
- service choices and tradeoffs

Be specific, but keep the slide titles outcome-led.

### Mixed Audience

Use a two-layer approach:

- core deck stays executive-friendly
- appendix or backup slides carry the deeper technical detail

### Internal Account Team

Optimize for:

- meeting objective
- stakeholder map
- likely objections
- recommended storyline
- next action

## Clarification Questions That Usually Matter

Ask only the questions that will materially change the deck.

High-value gaps usually include:

- audience and decision-maker level
- target workload or use case
- customer pain or urgency
- desired outcome of the meeting
- incumbent platform or competitor
- timeline and success criteria
- whether the ask is discovery, recommendation, or validation

## Recommendation Frame

When you present a recommendation, structure it like this:

- `Situation`
- `Recommended approach`
- `Why OCI fits`
- `Alternative`
- `Tradeoffs`
- `Assumptions`
- `Next step`

This keeps the deck honest and helps the user reuse the content in customer conversation.

## Objection Handling Patterns

### Why OCI?

Answer in customer terms first:

- better fit for the workload
- simpler operations through managed services
- security and governance posture
- migration path or coexistence advantages
- performance, scale, or data strategy fit

Do not answer with a raw list of OCI services.

### Is this too much change?

Use phased adoption language:

- start with the smallest valuable landing zone
- keep coexistence where needed
- validate with a POV or pilot
- reduce operational burden with managed services when appropriate

### What is the migration risk?

Show risk control:

- phased migration
- explicit dependencies
- rollback or coexistence path
- success criteria

### How do we prove value?

Translate the deck into a concrete follow-up:

- workshop
- POV or POC
- architecture assessment
- migration discovery
- executive readout

## Slide Writing Rules

- The slide title should state the takeaway, not just the topic.
- Keep support points to the smallest number that still proves the message.
- Use numbers only when they are sourced or explicitly given by the user.
- Prefer one visual and one takeaway over dense prose.
- If a slide needs more than one major idea, split it.

## Honesty Rules

- Separate what the customer said from what you are inferring.
- Separate what is currently known from what still needs validation.
- Do not overstate product maturity, pricing, region coverage, or operational simplicity.
- If the deck compares OCI to another platform, keep the comparison tied to the customer's workload and decision criteria.
